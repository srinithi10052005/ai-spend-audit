# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact target user

Not "startups." The exact user is an **engineering manager or CTO at a
B2B SaaS company with 3-15 engineers**, Series A or pre-Series A, who
personally approves the monthly AI tool subscriptions for their team.

They are technically literate enough to evaluate tools but too busy to
audit every line item. They signed up for Cursor when it launched, added
GitHub Copilot because a senior engineer asked, and now pay for both
without knowing if they need both. They have never compared the two.

They feel a low-grade guilt every month when the credit card statement
arrives but do not have time to investigate. This tool gives them the
answer in 2 minutes.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with zero budget

### Week 1 — Seed with real conversations (Days 1-7)

Do not post publicly yet. Instead:

1. **DM 20 engineering managers on LinkedIn** with a genuine message:
   "I built a free tool that audits AI tool spend in 2 minutes. Would you
   try it and tell me if the recommendations make sense to you?" No pitch,
   no ask beyond feedback. Target people at Series A companies with 5-20
   engineers based on LinkedIn search.

2. **Post in Indie Hackers** under "What I built this week" — not a launch
   post, a process post. "I built an AI spend auditor in 7 days — here is
   what I learned about how startups pay for AI tools." Link the tool at
   the bottom. Indie Hackers readers love process posts and will try the
   tool out of curiosity.

3. **Post in the Cursor Discord** — "I built a tool that tells you if
   Cursor Business is worth it vs Pro for your team size. Free, takes 2
   minutes." This is hyper-targeted — people in the Cursor Discord are
   actively thinking about Cursor pricing.

### Week 2 — First real distribution (Days 8-14)

4. **Post on Hacker News as Show HN**: "Show HN: Free AI tool spend
   auditor — find out if you're overpaying for Cursor, Claude, Copilot"
   The title is specific and useful. HN readers are exactly the target
   user. A Show HN that hits the front page delivers 500-2000 visitors
   in 24 hours.

5. **Write one tweet thread**: "We analyzed 50 AI tool audits. Here is
   what we found: [specific insight about overspend patterns]." Use real
   data from the first week of audits. Attach a screenshot of the results
   page. Tag a few AI tool accounts.

6. **Email 10 startup newsletters** that cover developer tools —
   Changelog, TLDR Tech, The Pragmatic Engineer. One sentence pitch:
   "Free tool your readers can use to audit their AI tool spend in 2
   minutes — no login required."

### Week 3-4 — Leverage the viral loop (Days 15-30)

7. **The shareable URL is the distribution.** Every audit creates a public
   URL. Users who share their results bring new users who run their own
   audit. Optimize the Open Graph preview to show the savings number
   prominently — "This team saves $340/mo on AI tools" is a compelling
   share.

8. **Comment on every HN thread about AI tool costs** with a genuine
   answer plus a link. "We actually built a tool that calculates this —
   here is the link." Do not spam. Only comment where it is genuinely
   relevant.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that oversold AI credits.
These companies — Cursor, Anthropic, OpenAI — have customers who are
churning or downgrading. Credex could partner with these vendors to offer
the audit tool as a value-add to their churning customers:

"Before you cancel, run a free audit to see if a different plan works
better for your team."

This is a channel no independent developer could access. Only Credex has
these vendor relationships.

## What week-1 traction looks like if this works

- 200+ audits completed
- 40+ email addresses captured (20% conversion from audit to email)
- 3+ organic shares of audit URLs on Twitter or Slack
- 1 Hacker News Show HN with 50+ upvotes
- 2-3 inbound messages from companies asking about Credex credits

If we hit 200 audits and 40 emails in week 1 with zero paid budget, the
unit economics work and we scale distribution.
