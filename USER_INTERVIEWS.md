# User Interviews

Three conversations with potential users conducted during the week of
May 8-14, 2026 through chat-based interactions and online discussions.
Responses were anonymized for privacy.

---

## Interview 1


**Name:** V. Janani  
**Role:** Student Developer  
**Company stage:** SDNB Vaishnav College for Women  
**Date:** 2026-05-07

**Direct quotes:**
- "Our team mostly uses ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot for daily work."
- "We have not checked the exact AI spending recently."
- "I would use this type of tool if it gives clear and secure suggestions."

**Most surprising thing they said:**  
The user mentioned that different people in the team buy subscriptions on
their own, so it becomes difficult to track the total spending.

**What it changed about my design:**  
This made me think about adding team-wise spending tracking and better
security information in the tool.

## Interview 2

**Name:** Anonymous respondent  
**Role:** Software Developer  
**Company stage:** Mid-size company  
**Date:** 2026-05-08

**Direct quotes:**
- "We mainly use ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot for coding."
- "Different people buy subscriptions separately, so we do not track total spending."
- "I would use a free tool that shows unused subscriptions."

**Most surprising thing they said:**  
Some employees pay for AI tools personally even when the company already
provides similar subscriptions.

**What it changed about my design:**  
I added the idea of detecting duplicate subscriptions and showing shared
team access.

## Interview 3

**Name:** Anonymous respondent  
**Role:** Freelance Designer and Content Creator  
**Company stage:** Freelancer  
**Date:** 2026-05-09

**Direct quotes:**
- "I mostly use ChatGPT and Canva Pro."
- "I forget about subscriptions until money gets deducted."
- "I would trust the tool if it explained savings clearly."

**Most surprising thing they said:**  
Users often continue using familiar tools even if cheaper alternatives are
available.

**What it changed about my design:**  
I decided the tool should explain recommendations clearly instead of only
focusing on saving money.
